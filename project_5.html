Project 5: Laser Cutting

Posted on December 11, 2023 by Ifigenia Oxyzolou

For this project, my goal was to laser cut a casing for the alarm clock I built in project 3!

The Design Process

My idea in approaching this project was to take the CAD design that I previously created for my alarm clock, and transform that into a design that could be laser cut. I was mainly concerned with the dimensions since I had already tested out and adjusted my design in project 3 to have the best fit for my alarm clock. The idea was that the main casing for the clock would be a laser-cut jointed box. Then a cover with holes for the display would nicely slide into the open side of the box to complete the casing.

Below is the dimensioned sketch for this concept. I shaved off approximately one millimeter from the dimensions of the cover piece in order to account for tolerance.

Once the concept was thought out, my first step was to design a jointed box on Fusion360. This was a quite tedious process that was made very approachable by the following youtube tutorial: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZrcqauNvt0M&t=1s. First I designed this basic box and then adjusted the dimensions according to the casing I designed for my alarm clock in project 3 (pictured in the sketch).

I then used those same previously established dimensions to design the cover for the face of the clock. I ensured that the thickness of my design was 3 mm to match that of the MDF particle board I was going to be laser cutting with.

The Laser Cutting Process

Once I had completed my Fusion360 designs, I downloaded the DXF files for each of the parts of the jointed box and the top piece and imported them to LightBurn. The layout and settings are pictured below.

Seeing my design be cut in real time was very satisfying, and provided much more instant gratification than 3D printing. The final result was successful and the pieces all fit together well. However, the parts needed hot gluing in order to stay together because the joints did not have enough friction between each other to stay put. Additionally, adding 1 mm for tolerance might have been a bit too much, and if I did this again I would have maybe changed it to around 0.5 mm.

I was very pleased overall with my first experience laser cutting and I would like to experiment more with this versatile tool in the future.
